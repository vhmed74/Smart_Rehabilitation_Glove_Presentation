Abstract

A 4-in-1 Therapeutic System

The system is fundamentally four devices integrated into one comprehensive platform, delivering a complete hand rehabilitation and assessment solution.

1. Hand Rehabilitation Mode
Pneumatic glove & pump system for mobility. Features 3 rate-based programs: 5, 10, or 15 open/close cycles per minute.
2. TENS Mode
Electrical stimulation for muscles and nerves. Offers 3 classic programs: Continuous, Burst, and Modulated.
3. Vibration Mode
Integrated vibration motor providing targeted muscle massage therapy to stimulate blood flow and reduce stiffness.
4. Force Measurement Module
Utilizes a force sensor to measure grip pressure, testing and displaying the patient's grip strength capacity as a percentage.
Clinical Value

Empowers therapists to monitor patients' progress quantitatively and adjust treatment intensity based on objective data — ensuring a personalized and documented recovery path.

Problem Statement

Core Challenges of Hand Rehabilitation

Overcoming functional and physical deficits that arise from injuries, diseases, or neurological conditions affecting the hand.

Limited Range of Motion

Fractures and arthritis restrict joint mobility, impairing the ability to grasp, pinch, or perform essential movements.

Muscle Atrophy & Weakness

Prolonged immobilization leads to muscle wasting, significantly reducing grip strength and endurance.

Sensory Deficits

Nerve damage alters tactile feedback (touch, temperature, pressure), making safe object manipulation difficult.

Impaired Coordination

Disrupted motor skills make fine, precise tasks such as writing, typing, or handling small objects extremely challenging.

Chronic Pain

Persistent pain acts as a major barrier, preventing patients from utilizing their hand effectively in daily life.

Our Smart Solution

An integrated wearable glove combining pneumatic actuation, TENS, vibration therapy, and real-time force measurement.

Historical Background

Evolution of Hand Rehabilitation

From ancient splinting to modern smart wearables — a journey spanning centuries of medical innovation.

Ancient Era
Ancient Beginnings

Early societies used primitive therapeutic methods like splinting and joint movement exercises in ancient Egypt to manage hand injuries.

18th–19th Century
Surgical Pioneers

Guillaume Dupuytren pioneered operative procedures for correcting hand deformities, establishing principles of modern rehabilitation.

World Wars
Wartime Innovation

Severe trauma catalyzed rapid advances: tendon rerouting, custom splint fabrication, and functional electrical stimulation (FES).

1950s–1977
Formal Specialty

Hand rehabilitation emerged as a formal specialty. The American Society of Hand Therapists (ASHT) was founded in 1977.

Today 2026

Components
Core Mode 1

Hand Rehabilitation Mode

A targeted pneumatic system designed for patients with neurological impairments, facilitating passive and active finger mobility.

Pneumatic Glove & Pump Actuation

The primary actuation mechanism uses a customized pneumatic pump to inflate and deflate air chambers within the glove, forcing the fingers into controlled flexion and extension.

3 Selectable Rate Programs

The software allows the therapist or patient to select between three specific rehabilitation rates depending on severity:
• 5 Cycles/Min: Slow rate for severe stiffness.
• 10 Cycles/Min: Moderate rate for standard therapy.
• 15 Cycles/Min: Fast rate for advanced recovery stages.

Core Mode 2

Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS)

Non-invasive electrotherapy for pain modulation, providing a safer alternative to pharmaceutical dependency.

01
Continuous Mode
Delivers a steady, uninterrupted flow of electrical impulses. Highly effective for acute pain relief by blocking pain signals via the Gate Control Theory.
02
Burst Mode
Delivers bursts of pulses followed by a rest period. Triggers the release of endorphins, making it ideal for chronic, deep muscle pain.
03
Modulated Mode
Constantly varies intensity and pulse width. This prevents nerve accommodation (body adapting to stimulus), ensuring long-term therapeutic efficacy.
Core Mode 3

Vibration & Massage Therapy

Targeted haptic feedback designed to stimulate blood circulation, reduce muscle spasticity, and enhance neuromuscular response.

ERM Haptic Actuators

Eccentric Rotating Mass (ERM) motors are strategically placed at specific pressure points along the hand and wrist to deliver deep tissue vibrations.

Dynamic PWM Control

The microcontroller utilizes Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) to adjust the vibration intensity in real-time, allowing for a personalized massage experience tailored to the patient's pain tolerance.

Core Mode 4

Force & Grip Measurement

Objective, quantitative assessment of hand recovery progress without relying on subjective patient feedback.

FSR Sensor Integration
Force Sensitive Resistors (FSR) are embedded in the glove to capture the exact mechanical pressure exerted by the patient's grip.
0 - 100% Capacity Scale
The analog sensor data is mapped to a highly readable percentage scale (0% to 100%). This allows the therapist to accurately test and track how much gripping force the patient has regained over time.
Materials & Methods — Core Components

Control, Power & Actuation

The electronic and mechanical backbone that drives the smart rehabilitation system.

Controller
ESP32 Microcontroller

Dual-core LX6 processor. Handles sensor reading, control logic, PWM generation, and wireless communication simultaneously.

Actuation
Pneumatic Glove

Wearable component with inflatable air actuators along each finger. Assists finger flexion and extension for stroke rehabilitation.

Interface
LCD Display Screen

Shows real-time system data: pressure levels, force %, valve status (open/closed), vibration intensity, and diagnostics.

Switching
MOSFET Module

High-speed electronic switch. Allows low-voltage ESP32 signal to safely control high-current loads (valves, motors) via PWM.

Power
Power Supply Adaptor

Converts 220V AC to DC (12V/5V). Protects circuit from voltage fluctuations and powers all electronic components safely.

Regulation
Power Supply Module (LM2596)

DC-DC step-down converter. Distributes stable voltages (3.3V, 5V, 12V) to match each component's specific power needs.

Hardware Subsystem

Pneumatic Control System

The core mechanical engine driving the passive hand rehabilitation cycles.

Miniature Air Pump (Compressor)

A highly efficient DC air pump generates the necessary positive and negative pressure to inflate and deflate the glove's air chambers seamlessly.

Solenoid Directional Valves

Electronically controlled 2-way and 3-way solenoid valves direct the airflow. They are rapidly switched by the microcontroller to alternate between the flexion (inflation) and extension (deflation) states.

Firmware & Software

Developed using Arduino IDE with C++. The ESP32 utilizes FreeRTOS capabilities on its dual cores to manage sensor polling, display updating, and wireless communication concurrently without blocking.

Hardware & Mechanical

Mechanical design and housing fabrication supported by CAD software (SolidWorks) for structural layout planning, ensuring a compact, ergonomic, and patient-friendly wearable format.

Patient Safety

Multi-Layered Safety Protocols

Ensuring the patient is never exposed to excessive pressure, shock, or mechanical strain during automated therapy sessions.

Auto Pressure Shutoff
The software continuously monitors the pneumatic pressure. If it exceeds the safe therapeutic threshold, the pump is instantly deactivated and exhaust valves open.
Electrical Isolation
The TENS module and main power supplies are optically isolated using relays and optocouplers to prevent any leakage current from reaching the patient.
Hardware Emergency Stop
A physical override switch allows the patient or therapist to instantly cut power to all actuators and deflate the glove in case of discomfort.
Testing & Validation

Rigorous 3-Stage
Test Strategy

All tests follow the V-model — each test case maps directly to a specific system requirement with pass/fail criteria.

ST1
Component-Level Testing (Unit)
13 components tested individually: MOSFET switching, 7805 regulation, valve activation, FSR linearity, HX710B pressure response, pneumatic airtightness.
ST2
Sub-System Integration Testing
Group interactions verified: ESP32 + MOSFET + Valve, ESP32 + FSR + Display, Power + Regulator chain. All protocols validated via oscilloscope.
ST3
System-Level Validation (End-to-End)
Complete clinical scenario: FSR press → valve open → glove inflate → pressure monitor → auto shutoff → display update. Looped 10+ cycles.
